Profile

Radiohead is an English rock band formed in 1985 in Abingdon, Oxfordshire. They're known for their experimental approach to rock music, incorporating elements of electronic, alternative, and art rock into their sound.

They gained widespread acclaim with their third album, "OK Computer" (1997), which is often considered one of the greatest albums of all time.

Members

Thom Yorke

Thom Yorke is the main vocalist and songwriter of Radiohead. He is described as one of the greatest and most influential singers of his generation.

Jonny Greenwood

Jonny Greenwood is the lead guitarist and keyboardist of Radiohead. He has been named one of the greatest guitarists by numerous publications.

Colin Greenwood

Nick O'Malley is the bassist of Radiohead. Along with bass guitar, Greenwood plays upright bass and electronic instruments.

Ed O'Brien

Ed O'Brien is the guitarist, songwriter, backing vocalist of Radiohead. He contributes to the band's upbeat live performances and gives the music a strong rhythm.

Philip Selway

Philip Selway is the drummer of Radiohead. He combines rock drumming with electronic percussion. Selway was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
